Overview

During Summer 2019, I participated in the first section of the summer Co-Op cohort of Moog in Blacksburg, Virginia. In, this I worked under the Space and Defense Group of Moog and developed algorithms for real-time image manipulation on FPGAs. I was interested in working on this project as it gave me the opportunity to begin learning computer vision and get familiar with FPGA programming. This position taught me important technical skills and gave me knowledge of various programs and design concepts relevant to Computer Vision and FPGA Programming. For this project, I was tasked with demonstrating the capability to perform real-time image manipulation on an FPGA, and then create a basic video processing program that future Moog projects that require computer vision can build upon. I was self-guided for the vast majority of the project, and had to find solutions through extensive research and study of documentation. By the end of the project I was able to code a program which performed Harris Corner analysis on video from a camera at 50 frames per second on the FPGA, improving 10x on the software implementation of the same algorithm. I also was able to make a template for coding Computer Vision algorithms on the FPGA that can be used in future projects. Overall, the experience gave me critical technical skills in relevant software, tested my critical thinking ability, and gave me an opportunity to learn FPGA hardware and design.

Personal Statement

My Motivations

I hope to use my computer engineering degree to build innovative devices such as exoskeletons and robotic assistive technologies. I aspire to be an engineer to build solutions and apply them to helping the lives of others. I was previously an Algorithm Developer and Diagram Designer for Scriyb, Laboratory Research Assistant at George Mason University, and Engineering Lead for the ASSIP Inventor's Club. Through these experiences, I have been a contributing author on a chapter of a book titled "Learning Engineering for Online Education", invented and patented two online education technologies, researched and built a testing mechanism for human motion, and created a device that monitors patient's knees after knee surgery to check for complications such as blood clots or improper gait.

These experiences are just the start of my journey as an engineer, and I'm excited to create many other innovations to assist those in need.
